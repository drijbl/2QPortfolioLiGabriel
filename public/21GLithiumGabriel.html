About De La Salle University

De La Salle University (DLSU) is a prestigious private Catholic research university located in Manila, Philippines. Established in 1911, it is part of the De La Salle Philippines network of Lasallian educational institutions. DLSU is renowned for its strong emphasis on science, technology, engineering, and business programs. As of 2025, it is ranked 2nd in the Philippines, 1681st globally, and 485th in Asia according to EduRank rankings.

Short History

De La Salle University (DLSU) was founded in 1911 by the Brothers of the Christian Schools in Paco, Manila. It was the first La Salle school established outside Europe and North America. The university later moved to Taft Avenue in 1921, where it continued to thrive as one of the leading private institutions in the Philippines.

In 1973, DLSU became coeducational and has since expanded its academic programs in engineering, business, science, and liberal arts. Its Lasallian mission focuses on faith, service, and commune, helping students to serve as competent profressionals and holistic agents of change.

Core Values

Lasalle has three (3) main core values that guide its mission:

  • Faith – Integration of faith and life, guided by moral integrity and spirituality.
  • Service – Commitment to serve others, especially the marginalized, with compassion and creativity.
  • Communion – Unity and collaboration in pursuing the Lasallian goal of transformative education.
